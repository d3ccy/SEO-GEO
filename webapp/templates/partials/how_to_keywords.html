How to use this tool ▼

How to use

Enter a seed keyword (e.g. "content strategy" or "cyber security consultancy"), choose a location, and set the result limit. Click Research Keywords.

Results appear as a sortable table — click any column header to sort. Use Export CSV to download all results for spreadsheet use or client presentations.

Load a Client Profile to auto-fill the keyword field with the client's domain as a seed.

Understanding the columns

Volume
Monthly Google searches for this keyword. Higher = more opportunity (and usually more competition).
Difficulty (0–100)
How hard to rank organically. Under 30 = low competition, 70+ = very hard.
CPC
Cost-per-click if advertising. High CPC = strong commercial value — useful for prioritisation even if you're not running ads.
Intent chips
Informational (research), Navigational (finding a site), Commercial (comparing options), Transactional (ready to act). Transactional + Commercial = highest conversion potential.
AI Volume
Estimated searches within AI platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity). High AI Volume = high GEO opportunity. Shown when the DataForSEO AI plan is active.

Quick tips

  • Sort by AI Volume to find keywords where GEO (not just SEO) is the opportunity
  • Informational intent = best target for GEO content — AI engines answer these by citing sources
  • A low-volume keyword with high AI Volume may be more valuable for GEO than a high-volume keyword with none
  • Export to CSV and paste into a spreadsheet to filter and prioritise for client strategy presentations
  • Larger result sets (50) take longer and use more API credit — use 20 for initial exploration

Common questions

Why is AI Volume blank for some keywords?
AI Volume requires the DataForSEO AI Optimization API plan. If you see dashes, that plan is not active on the current credentials.
What does "N/A" mean for Difficulty?
The DataForSEO bulk difficulty API did not return a score for that keyword. Can happen for very niche or newly-trending terms.
Can I research keywords for a different country?
Yes — use the Location selector. Volume and competition data are geo-specific. The default is United Kingdom.
